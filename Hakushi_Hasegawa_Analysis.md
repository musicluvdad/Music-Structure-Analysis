## 1. The End of "Avant-Garde" as a Missed Swing
[span_5](start_span)The "avant-garde" in 20th-century music relied on a narrative of human struggle—the "wind pressure of a missed swing"—against existing systems[span_5](end_span). [span_6](start_span)Hakushi Hasegawa neutralizes this by treating complex structures not as challenges, but as initial parameters, similar to lighting or opacity settings in Blender[span_6](end_span). [span_7](start_span)This precision purges the "human heat" once central to the avant-garde, reducing their history of struggle to a blank slate[span_7](end_span).

## 2. 毒 (Doku): Forced Access and Ritual Synchronization
[span_8](start_span)The performance begins with **毒 (Doku)**, a forced access to the "Hasegawa System"[span_8](end_span). [span_9](start_span)Every keystroke is synchronized with 1-frame precision to colorful, poppy physical computing objects in Blender[span_9](end_span). [span_10](start_span)This synchronization is not an accompaniment but a ritual to write a new protocol into the observer's brain, transforming the human performer into a high-performance input device[span_10](end_span).

## 3. 砂漠で (Sabaku de): Utopia of Stasis via Information Overload
[span_11](start_span)In **砂漠で (Sabaku de)**, the forward momentum of the breakbeats reaches a critical density, resulting in absolute stasis—a "Steady-State Plasma Universe" where time disappears[span_11](end_span). [span_12](start_span)This is a 2020s redefinition of absolute zero transparency, where the tranquility after the calculation is perfectly solved represents the true nature of Hasegawa's structural catharsis[span_12](end_span).

## 4. Blender Physical Computing: The Externalized Embodiment

## 5. Coordinates of a Liberated and Healthy Post-Human
[span_16](start_span)Hasegawa’s music is characterized by the total exclusion of pathological "dampness" or closure[span_16](end_span).
