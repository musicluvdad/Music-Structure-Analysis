### Abstract
This critique re-examines Hakushi Hasegawa's *Mahou Gakko* as a deliberate "destructive testing" of musical perception. By moving beyond Newtonian organicism into the "gravitational sphere of a collapsing star," the album dismantles the listener's safety through "gravitational collapse beats" and strategic sound pressure manipulation. The work functions as a "re-education facility" where violent sonic storms are misperceived as "excessive euphoria," achieving an irreversible alteration of perception.

### 1. The Trap of Evaluation: Beyond "Pop Chaos"
Public evaluations often praise the work as a "high-level fusion of pop and complexity" or the "pinnacle of modern pop music". However, these interpretations merely trace the superficial "pop design" strategically set by Hasegawa. To truly understand this work, one must transition from perceiving "Newtonian order" to the "gravitational sphere of a collapsing star".

### 3. Core Mechanisms: Gravitational Collapse Beats
The "singularity" of this work is defined by the transformation of rhythm into **Gravitational Collapse Beats**.
* **Compression:** Snare and bass drum kicks are fired in succession to "paint over" the sonic surface, crushing any sense of openness.
* **Rigidity:** The intense sound image forces a "rigidity" upon the listener, making movement impossible within the magnetic storm.
* **Radiation:** The bright melodies heard while being sucked into the event horizon are radiations of "excessive euphoria" emitted from the point of collapse.

### 4. Selected Field Investigation Records
* **`Departed`**: The entrance to "Glitter Grind"—a world where percussive convulsions and rigidity coexist.
* **`Mouth Flash`**: Camouflaged gaiety. Hard snares function as "interrupt processing," leading to mental attrition via physical pressure.
* **`Kyofu no Hoshi (Stellar Terror)`**: The maximum output gravity source. It utilizes death metal-style blasts to hack the listener's senses, forcing them to misperceive terminal violence as "pleasant acceleration".
* **`Enbami`**: A system-wide initialization (System Format) that uses roaring sound and noise to forcibly clear the listener's overflowed brain.

### 5. Operation: Sensory Reconstruction (The "Accomplice" Effect)
The album's sequence is a designed "Destructive Test" intended to corner the listener. Through a relentless cycle of relaxation (harmonies) and impact (gravitational collapse), the brain is forced to adapt to high-energy sound images.
* **Adaptation:** When a listener finds exhilaration in violent breakbeats, they cease to be a "subject" and become an **"accomplice"** of the warped spacetime.

### Conclusion: The Re-education Facility
The title *Mahou Gakko* (Magic School) is an ironic metaphor for a **re-education facility** meant to adapt listeners to an environment where existing physical laws of pop music no longer apply. The final track, "Outside," offers no return to peace, but rather a state of "contaminated rest". Survivors become addicts of the abnormal high-energy state, seeking the singularity of "Departed" in an eternal, circular recurrence.
