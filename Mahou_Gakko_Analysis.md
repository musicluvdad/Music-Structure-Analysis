### Abstract
This critique re-examines Hakushi Hasegawa's *Mahou Gakko* as a deliberate "destructive testing" of musical perception. By moving beyond Newtonian organicism into the "gravitational sphere of a collapsing star," the album dismantles the listener's safety through "gravitational collapse beats" and strategic sound pressure manipulation. The work functions as a "re-education facility" where violent sonic storms are misperceived as "excessive euphoria," achieving an irreversible alteration of perception itself.

### 1. The Trap of Evaluation: Beyond "Pop Chaos"
Public evaluations often praise the work as a "high-level fusion of pop and complexity". However, these interpretations merely trace the superficial "pop design" strategically set by Hasegawa. To truly understand this work, one must transition from perceiving "Newtonian order" to the "gravitational sphere of a collapsing star".

### 2. Evolution of the Spacetime Model
The transition across Hasegawa's discography represents a fundamental shift in the listener's role from "observer" to "concerned party".

* **Phase I: *Kusaki Moudo***
    * **Physical Model:** Newtonian Mechanics (Organic cycles and movement).
    * **Listener Status:** Observer at a safe distance.
* **Phase II: *Air Ni Ni***
    * **Physical Model:** Steady-State Plasma (Self-organization via Birkeland currents).
    * **Listener Status:** Distant admirer of cosmic phenomena.
* **Phase III: *Mahou Gakko***
    * **Physical Model:** Gravitational Collapse (Plasma instability and the Event Horizon).
    * **Listener Status:** Inescapable entrapment in a magnetic storm.

### 3. Core Mechanisms: Gravitational Collapse Beats
The "singularity" of this work is defined by the transformation of rhythm into **Gravitational Collapse Beats**.
* **High-Density Compression:** Snare and bass drum kicks are fired in rapid succession as if painting over a surface, crushing the previous sense of openness.
* **Structural Rigidity:** The intense sound image forces a "rigidity" upon the listener, as if movement is inhibited by a powerful magnetic storm.
* **Radiation of Euphoria:** Bright melodies appearing at the event horizon are radiations of "excessive euphoria" emitted from the ultimate point of collapse.

### 4. Selected Field Investigation Records
* **`Departed`**: The entrance to "Glitter Grind" where convulsions and rigidity coexist.
* **`Mouth Flash`**: A cheerful samba melody camouflaging underlying violence. Hard snares function as "interrupt processing," leading to mental attrition via physical pressure.
* **`Kyofu no Hoshi (Stellar Terror)`**: The maximum output gravity source. While the soundscape remains pop, it adopts the structural logic of death metal via the relentless alternating motion of blast beats and beatdowns. This contrast in bass drum kicks generates a physical propulsion that hacks the listener's senses, causing them to misperceive lethal violence as "pleasant acceleration".
* **`Enbami`**: A system-wide initialization (System Format) that uses roaring sound and voice installations to physically destroy musical consistency.

### 5. Operation: Sensory Reconstruction (The "Accomplice" Effect)
The album's sequence is a designed "Destructive Test" intended to corner the listener. Through a relentless cycle of relaxation (harmonies) and impact (gravitational collapse), the brain is forced to adapt to high-energy sound images.
* **Adaptation:** When a listener finds exhilaration in violent breakbeats, they cease to be a "subject" and have been remade into an **"accomplice"** of the warped spacetime.

### Conclusion: The Re-education Facility
The title *Mahou Gakko* is an ironic metaphor for a **re-education facility** meant to adapt listeners to an environment where the context of pop music no longer apply. The final track, "Outside," offers no return to the original world, but a state of "contaminated rest". Survivors become addicts of the abnormal high-energy state, seeking the singularity of "Departed" in an eternal, circular recurrence.
